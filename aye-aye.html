BRANDING

The aye-aye (Daubentonia madagascariensis) is a strepsirrhine primate endemic to Madagascar, related to lemurs. His outlandish appearance makes him considered the main person responsible for the origin of the word "lemur", which means "night spirit" in Latin. Aye Aye Restaurant has that happy and vibrant spirit during the day, but also that party spirit at night. At Aye Aye Bar Restaurant, Aye Aye is the icon that represents our culture of service and satisfaction, but also represents the pride and values of Madagascar, which through its dishes and drinks we want to share.

The logo proposes a reasonably colorful color palette, which aims to draw the attention of consumers both day and night. The logo shows the face of the mascot, the Aye Aye, as the main element. However, it also proposes a typeface called "Jungle Tribe" its objective is to evoke elements or part of the surrounding wild environment in which the restaurant's mascot lives most of the time. We will be able to observe the colors used both for the mascot as well as for the typography and vector elements. The logo was for me as a designer a changing element and that can still come to propose variants, which in turn could even exceed the final design.

LOGO & ALTERNATIVES

As we had already mentioned before, the logo was something that I had a lot of fun with, since I was able to create different alternatives, which I fell in love with many of, however, when asking for opinions from other experts in the field, they advised me to play nothing but with a few, which I will present below. The logos that are presented on this occasion, as you can see, present colors related to the palette shown above as well as related to posters and menus. The opinions provided during the process help me, of course, to simulate what a client would like when providing their opinion and subsequent wishes for changes. As graphic designers we must be flexible and act according to the needs of our clients, as professionals we must not informally interfere with the desires and needs of our clients, but formally advise with our experience, with the aim of enriching their proposals.
